Everyone tells you to write like this:

Short lines.

Like this.

Full stop after each one.

It's "mobile optimised" and "easy to read".

It's also annoying to read if your audience is smart and perfectly capable of processing a full paragraph.

That formatting style exists for a reason:

It works for broad audiences who typically read at a lower level. But if you're writing for founders, executives, people with higher reading abilities and the desire to actually read things, you're optimising for the wrong reader. And you might be actively putting off the right one.

Same goes for walls of text, long hooks, emojis. 

None of it is universally good or bad. The question is never "does this trick work?", it's "does this trick work for my audience?"

Think about what a formatting rule is actually designed to do before you blindly apply it.

Different playbooks for different people.
